How People Think About AI

Before we begin

This is a study about how people think about and relate to AI assistants. By "AI assistants" we mean conversational tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and similar products that you can ask questions or give tasks to in plain language.

Have you used an AI assistant before?

The study takes about 10 to 12 minutes. There are no right or wrong answers.

Required to proceed.

Adult Consent Form

Purpose of the study. This study is being conducted by Alison Gopnik, professor of psychology at the University of California, Berkeley, and her laboratory of research assistants, graduate students, and post-doctoral fellows. We invite you to participate in a study of how adults perceive and define artificial intelligence (AI) as well as how they use it. We are also interested in how their creativity defines how they use AI. Participation is entirely voluntary. If anything is not clear, please do not hesitate to ask questions.

Study procedures. There will be 1 testing session that will last no longer than 20 minutes. You will be asked to complete a short survey and an interactive task involving an AI assistant. You will make all decisions about whether to seek AI assistance and what to do next. You may refrain from answering any questions that make you uncomfortable and may withdraw your participation at any time even after completing the experiment without penalty.

Benefits. While there is no direct benefit to you, you may enjoy the novelty of the task. You will also be making an important contribution to improving our understanding of how people think about and interact with AI.

Risks and discomforts. We do not expect you to face any foreseeable risks from your participation. There is the unlikely possibility that your confidentiality will be compromised; however, we have taken measures to minimize this risk.

Confidentiality. Your identity will be separated from your data and a random numerical code will be assigned to keep track of the data. Your confidentiality will be kept to the degree permitted by the technology being used. No guarantees can be made regarding the interception of data sent via the Internet by any third parties. Data will be kept indefinitely. All information will be securely stored at the University in encrypted format. Only laboratory personnel will have access to this data. No individuals will be identified in any presentation or publication. Identifiers might be removed from identifiable private information. After such removal, the information could be used for future research studies or distributed to other investigators for future research studies without additional informed consent from you.

Compensation. You will receive compensation as described in the study listing. This study contains a number of checks to make sure that participants are finishing the tasks honestly and completely. As long as you read the instructions and complete the tasks, your submission will be approved. If you fail these checks, your submission will be rejected and you will not be compensated.

Rights. Your participation in the research is voluntary and you are free to withdraw your consent and discontinue participation at any time. Whether or not you participate in this study will have no bearing on your status with respect to any program, department, or service at the University of California, and will involve no adverse consequences.

Questions. If you have any questions or concerns, please contact the study team. If you have any questions regarding your rights as a participant in this research project, please contact the Committee for the Protection of Human Subjects at the University of California at Berkeley at (510) 642-7461 or subjects@berkeley.edu.
